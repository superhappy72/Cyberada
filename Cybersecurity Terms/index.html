Placeholder page terms and glossary.

Exposure
Extent to which an organization and/or stakeholder is subject to a risk; The combination of likelihood and impact levels for a risk.

Intrusion
A security event that constitutes a security incident in which an intruder attempts to gain access to a system without having authorization to do so.

Risk Mitigation
Prioritizing, evaluating, and implementing the appropriat risk-reducing controls/countermeasures recommended from the risk managment process. A subset of risk response

Honeypot
A decoy system or resource designed to attract and trap potential attackers

IOT: Internet of Things
Everyday objects that require a network connection.

Backdoor
A backdoor is a way to gain unauthorized access to a computer system, allowing users to bypass normal security measures and gain high level user access. From there, attackers could potentially steal personal data, install unwanted software, or hijack devices.

Ciphertext
Data in its encrypted from.

Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)
An adversary with sophisticated levels of expertise and significant resources, allowing it through the use of multiple different attack vectors (E.g., cyber physical, and deception) to generate opportunities to establish and extend its presence within the information technology infrastructure of organizations for purposes of continually exfilrating information and or to undermine or impede critical aspects of a mission, program, or organization, or place itself in a position to do so in the future; moreover. the advanced persistent threat pursues its objectives repeatedly opver an extended period of time, adapting to a defenders efforts to resist it, and with determination to maintain the level of interaction needed to execute its objectivel.

Malware
Hardware, Firmwar, or software that is intentionally included or inserted in a system for harmful purpose.

Encryption
The process of usuing Ciphertext* to convert plaintext into encrpted state to protect important info. The reverse of encryption is decryption: converting encrypted text into plaintext.
Handshake
Protocol dialouge between two systems for indentifying and authenticating themselves to each other, or for synchronizing their operations with eachother.
